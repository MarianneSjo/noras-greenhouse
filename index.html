~ the seasons ~

A garden journal

Small notes from the greenhouse, across the turning year.

Early Spring

Seedlings awake

The first tomatoes and paprika seedlings are up. Basil is sensitive to the cool spring nights — keeping them close to the warmth of the greenhouse centre.

Late Spring

Ready to transplant

Cherry tomatoes and cucumbers sized up nicely. Rucola and New Zealand spinach already giving first harvests. The spring onions look decorative enough to leave in the pots.

Summer

Abundance

The Cherry Baby tomatoes are living up to their name — genuinely sweet. Pinching the side shoots weekly. Cucumbers producing faster than I can pick them.

Autumn

Winter prep

Freezing New Zealand spinach for winter storage. Harvesting rhubarb. The Victoria variety really is hardy — svært hardfør, as the sign says.

~ about ~

Why I garden

It started small — a few pots, a cherry tomato plant that did surprisingly well — and grew from there. Now the greenhouse holds more than I can eat, which is part of the point.

There's something grounding about the rhythm of it. Seeds to seedlings to harvest, repeating each year but never quite the same. Some years the paprika is generous, some years the basil sulks through a cold spring.

I write on the plant signs by hand because it slows me down. Latin names, growing notes, what each plant likes. The signs are for me, really — but visitors seem to enjoy reading them too.

— Nora
